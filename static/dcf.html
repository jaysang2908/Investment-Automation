DCF Calculator — How It Works
Select any stock you have generated a report for and adjust the valuation inputs at your discretion. Base inputs are pre-loaded from the generated DCF model, which already incorporates analyst estimates on revenue and key metrics. Both valuation methods use Unlevered Free Cash Flow (UFCF) for the 5-year projection period — choose mid-year or end-of-year discounting convention.
Gordon Growth Method (GGM)
Values the company by discounting projected cash flows into perpetuity using a Terminal Growth Rate (TGR). Best suited to stable, cash-generative businesses. Reference points for TGR are auto-generated based on historical revenue growth and sector norms.
Earnings Multiple Method (EM)
Values the company by applying a terminal multiple to an anchor metric — UFCF, EBITDA, Earnings, FCF, or OCF. Select the multiple that best reflects how the market prices the sector. Trailing multiples are shown as guardrails.
Guardrails & Reference Points
Average historical growth rates and trailing valuation multiples are auto-generated alongside inputs to anchor your assumptions. Scenario saves allow you to compare bear, base, and bull cases side by side.
Due Diligence: Base inputs reflect the generated DCF model. All adjustments are at the user's discretion — cross-check outputs against current market data and company filings for reasonableness before drawing conclusions.
